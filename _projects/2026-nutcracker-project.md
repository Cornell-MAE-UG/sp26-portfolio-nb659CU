1.
Find: Dimensions of nut cracker to obtain cracking load for macadamia nut for average grip strength

2.
Given:
avg. load to crack macadamia nut: 222 kgf
50th percentile female grip strength ages 20-24: 28.6 kgf (data from: PMID 39647778)
average whole macadamia nut diameter: approx. 25mm

3.
Assume that the nut is being compressed near the joint at the summit of the nut cracker, at a distance = radius + 1.5 mm
The forces of the hand that is compressing the handle and the normal reaction forces of the nut are acting at the same moment arm about the pin the of the nutcracker. Therefore, the project consisted of finding the dimensions (i.e. the distance between the two forces) to create a mechanical advantage enough to reach the load required to crack the nut.
Let x be the desired distance: x = ((14mm)*(111kgf))/((14.3kgf)) = 109mm

5.
The device is usable according to the calculations. It should be simple for most men and a large portion of women between the ages of 20 and 50; however, especially for the latter, it may take almost all of the their grip force which could be a little uncomfortable. The dimensions are also resonable maxing out at about 10cm.
